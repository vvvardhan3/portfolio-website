Life Stages:

Childhood Days

My childhood was like a science experiment gone hilariously wrong! Academics? Let's just say I gave them a run for their money. While others were mastering ABCs, I was busy trying to understand my own version of a mysterious chemical equation. Yes, those chemicals that every Indian house has, I mixed them like a mad scientist. Sparks flew, but not in the way you'd expect!. I tried building my own electronic motors - little did I know they'd spin me in circles. Batteries became my friends, and I learned that when they ran out of juice, they're worse than grumpy morning people. I also dabbled with lights, trying to make them blink like a disco party for ants!


I was a master of playing with electrical sockets. Some kids had dolls, I had sockets. I'd stare at them like they were a puzzle waiting to be solved. I even tried reverse engineering my electronic toys because why not? And learning my mother tongue, Telugu? Oh boy, my tongue had a mind of its own! But hey, my passion for music matched my passion for mangling Telugu, so it was a good trade-off. Cricket, well, that was my second religion. I played, watched, and probably dreamed of becoming a cricketer one day. My friends couldn't decide if I was more obsessed with hitting a six or singing a song!
